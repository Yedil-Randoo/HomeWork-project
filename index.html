01. Project Genesis

HomeworkTracker was born from a simple observation: existing platforms are built for teachers, not students. We needed a tool that prioritizes the user's workload over administrative data.

This system bridges the gap between chaotic physical planners and overly complex corporate software, providing a streamlined "Success Hub" for every student.

The core Problem

With an average of 12 subjects per term, tracking individual task requirements is a nightmare. Information gets lost in chats and emails.

The Fix: A persistent web-based dashboard accessible from any school device, keeping your priorities visible at all times.

03. Full Transcript Interview

What does your product consist of?
Our product allows students to manually record assignments, including subject names, specific tasks, and their respective deadlines for the week.
Is this application restricted to students?
While students are our primary users, the tool is a universal planner. Adults can use it for professional goals or certificate courses, though our UI is optimized for school workflows.
Why did you opt for a website over a mobile app?
Classroom dynamics played a huge role. Since phones are often collected by teachers, having a web tool accessible via laptops ensures students can stay organized during lessons.
How is the data organized within the system?
We use deadline-priority sorting. However, we are actively developing a "Goal-Based" view and a calendar layout to give users a better overview of their monthly schedule.
Can users track their completion progress?
Yes. We integrated a completion toggle. Being able to "check off" a task is vital for psychological motivation and clear progress tracking.
What about automated notifications?
That's the next frontier. It’s technically challenging for a basic web app, but we are researching service workers to enable push notifications for upcoming deadlines.
